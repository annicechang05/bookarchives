My Top 10 Books

These books are special to me, each in its own way. Some sparked my love for a genre, others pulled me in with beautiful writing or unforgettable characters. A few stayed with me long after the last page, not just for the stories they told, but for the emotions they stirred.

Beach Read

By. Emily Henry

This is the book that got me into reading and my introduction to Emily Henry, my favorite author. Her writing has so much depth and substance, and always bring me to tears. This was a perfectly paced slow-burn that had me completely invested in both characters.

Just For The Summer

By. Abby Jimenez

This was my favorite book of 2024. Justin is the epitome of a perfect book boyfriend, and is definitely the standard. There is a quote in this book, that is far too long to include, but describes love so beautifully. 10/10, no notes.

The Nightingale

By. Kristin Hannah

I've never cried over a book as much as I did for this. Kristin Hannah's writing brought every single moment to life and I felt like I knew the characters intimately and was experiencing the war with them.

Daisy Jones and the Six

By. Taylor Jenkins Reid

One of the most unique books I've read. It's entirely written as an interview, so we get all of the character's POVs. This sucked me into the story and the writing was so good that the band felt like they were real.

Dance of Thieves

By. Mary E. Pearson

This book consists of my favorite tropes in fantasy-romance books: fake dating, enemies to lovers, duty versus love, etc. These tropes came together perfectly in this book, and is definitely the blueprint for books such as the Powerless trilogy.

Happy Place

By. Emily Henry

While I did find the miscommunication a little frustrating, I loved the setting of this book, as well as the found family aspect. And again, I was rooting for the characters. Favorite quote: "In every universe, it’s you for me. Even if it’s not me for you.”

The Blood Traitor

By. Lynette Noni

Such an underrated series. This is the final book in the Prison Healer trilogy, and I found myself attatched to the characters by the end of it. This series contained unpredicatable cliff-hangers that made it so bingeable. Cried a few times for our characters.

Beartown

By. Fredrik Backman

This is a book that will stay with me for a very long time. This book was so well written, and Fredrik Backman's writing style kept me so engaged in the book, and wondering what was going to happen to the characters.

Summer Romance

By. Annabel Monaghan

Annabel Monaghan is able to craft banter and chemistry between characters that feels palpable off the page. The writing is so comforting and I love the way the story is wrapped together into one big message at the end.

An Ember in the Ashes

By. Sabaa Tahir

I don't remember much about this book, I read it a few years ago. However, I do remember loving it and reading all four books in a week. It was fast paced and I loved the characters, as well as the romance between Elias and Laia.
